​‍​​​‍‍​​‍‍​‍‍‍​​‍​‍‍​​‍​‍​‍‍​​​​‍​‍​​‍​​‍‍‍​‍‍​​‍‍​​​‍‍​‍‍​‍​​‍​‍​​‍​​‍​​‍‍​‍‍​​‍​​‍​​‍​‍‍​‍​‍​​‍​​​​​‍​‍‍‍​‍‍‍​‍​​‍‍​‍​‍​‍​‍​​​‍​​​‍​‍​​‍‍​‍​‍​‍​​‍‍​‍​‍​‍​‍​​​‍​​​‍​‍​‍‍‍‍​​​​‍​​‍‍​‍​‍​​​‍​​​‍​​​‍​‍​‍‍‍​‍‍‍​‍​​‍‍​‍​‍‍​‍​‍‍​​‍‍​​​‍​‍​​​​‍​​‍​‍​​‍​​‍​​​‍​​​‍​‍​‍​‍​​‍‍​​​‍​‍​‍​‍‍​​‍​‍​‍​​​‍‍​‍‍​​​‍​​‍​​‍​‍​​‍‍​‍​‍​​​‍​‍​‍​‍‍​​‍​‍‍‍‍​​​​‍​​‍‍​‍​‍​​​‍​​​‍​​​​​‍​‍‍‍​‍‍‍​‍​​‍‍​‍​‍‍​‍​​‍​‍​​‍​​‍​‍‍‍​​‍‍​‍​​‍​​‍​‍‍​‍‍​​​‍​​​​‍​​‍‍‍‍​​‍​‍‍​​​‍​​​‍‍​​‍‍​‍​​​​‍​​‍‍​‍​​​​‍​‍‍​‍​​​‍‍​​‍​​‍​​​‍‍​​​‍‍​​​​​‍​‍‍​‍​​‍​‍​‍​‍​‍‍​‍‍​​​‍​​​‍​‍​‍​​‍​​‍​‍‍​‍​‍​​‍‍​‍‍‍‍​‍‍​‍​​‍​‍​​‍‍‍​​‍‍​‍‍​‍​‍​‍​​​‍​‍‍‍‍​​‍​‍​​‍‍​‍​‍​​​‍‍‍​‍​‍‍​‍​​‍‍​‍​​​​‍​​‍‍​‍​‍​‍​‍‍‍​‍​​​‍​‍​‍‍‍​‍​​​‍​‍‍​‍​​‍‍​‍​‍​​‍​​​​​‍​‍‍‍​‍‍‍​‍​‍‍​​‍​‍​‍​​‍‍​​‍‍​​​​​​‍‍​​​​​‍​‍‍​‍​​‍​​​‍​​​‍​‍​‍​‍​​‍‍​‍​‍​‍​​‍‍​​​‍​‍​‍‍‍​‍​​​‍​‍​​‍‍​‍​‍​‍​​‍‍​‍​‍‍​‍​‍​​‍​‍​​​‍​‍‍‍​‍​​​‍​​‍‍‍​​‍‍‍‍​‍​​‍​‍‍​‍​​‍‍​‍​​​​‍​​‍‍​‍​‍‍‍‍​‍​​‍‍​‍​​​​‍‍​‍​‍​​‍​​‍‍‍​​‍​‍​‍‍‍​‍​‍​​​‍​‍‍‍‍​​‍​‍​​‍‍​‍​‍​‍​‍​​​‍​‍​​‍​​‍‍​‍​​‍​‍​​‍​​‍​‍‍​‍​​‍​‍‍‍​‍‍‍​‍‍​‍​​‍​‍​‍​‍​‍​‍‍​‍‍​‍​‍​‍​‍‍​​‍‍‍‍​‍​​‍​‍‍​‍​​‍​‍‍​​​​‍​​‍​‍​​​‍‍​​‍​​‍​‍‍​‍​​‍​‍​‍‍‍​‍​‍​​‍​​‍​​​‍​​​‍‍​​​‍​​​‍‍​​‍​​‍​‍​​‍​​‍‍​‍‍​​​‍​​‍‍​‍​‍‍​‍​​‍​‍​​‍​​‍​​‍‍​‍‍​​‍​​‍​​‍​‍‍​‍‍​‍​‍​​‍‍‍​​‍‍​‍​‍‍​‍​‍‍​​‍​‍‍​‍‍​‍​‍​‍​​​‍​​‍‍​‍​‍​‍​​‍‍​‍​‍​​​‍​​​‍‍​‍​​​​‍‍​‍​‍​​‍​​‍‍​​​‍​‍​‍‍‍​‍​‍​​​‍​‍‍‍​‍‍‍​‍​​‍‍​‍​‍‍‍‍​‍​​‍​​‍‍​‍​‍‍‍​‍​​​‍​​‍‍‍​​‍​​​‍​​​‍​​‍‍​‍​‍‍‍​‍‍‍​‍​​‍‍‍​​‍‍‍‍​​‍​​‍‍​​​‍​‍‍​‍​​​​‍​‍‍​‍​​‍‍​‍‍​‍​‍​‍‍​‍​​‍‍​‍‍​‍​‍​​‍‍​​​‍​‍​‍​​​‍‍​‍​‍‍​‍‍‍​‍‍‍​‍​‍‍​‍​​‍​​​‍‍‍​‍​‍​​‍​​‍‍​‍​‍‍​‍​​‍‍‍‍​‍​‍​‍‍‍​‍​‍‍​​‍​‍‍‍‍​​‍​‍​‍‍​‍​​‍‍​‍‍​‍​‍​​​‍​‍​​‍‍​‍​‍​‍​​‍‍‍‍​‍​‍​​‍‍​‍​​‍​‍​​​‍‍‍​​‍​‍‍​​‍‍​​‍​‍​​​‍​​‍‍‍‍​‍​​‍‍‍‍​‍Most people dislike their jobs. It's an astonishing but statistical fact. // 大多数人不喜欢自己的工作。这是一个令人惊讶但确凿的事实。
A primary cause of employee dissatisfaction, according to fresh research, is that many believe they have terrible managers. // 根据最新研究，员工不满的一个主要原因是许多人认为他们的管理者很糟糕。
Few describe their managers as malicious or manipulative, though. While those types certainly exist, they are minority. // 很少有人将管理者描述为恶意或操纵型的人。虽然这类人确实存在，但他们是少数。
The majority of managers seemingly just don't know any better. They're often emulating bad managers they've had in the past. // 大多数管理者似乎只是不知道更好的方式。他们经常模仿过去遇到过的糟糕管理者。
It's likely they've never read a management book or attended a management course. They might not have even reflected on what good management looks like and how it would influence their own management style. // 他们很可能从未读过管理书籍或参加过管理课程。他们甚至可能从未思考过什么是好的管理，以及这会如何影响他们自己的管理风格。
The researchers interviewed employees about their managers, beginning with a question about the worst manager they had ever had. From this, the researchers came up with four main causes of why some managers are perceived as being simply awful at their jobs. // 研究人员就管理者问题采访了员工，首先问的是他们遇到过的最糟糕的管理者。由此，研究人员总结出为什么有些管理者被认为在工作中表现极差的四个主要原因。
The first cause was company culture, which was seen by employees as enabling poor management practices. It was specifically stressful work environments, minimal training and a lack of accountability that were found to be the most blameworthy. // 第一个原因是公司文化，员工认为它助长了不良的管理实践。具体来说，压力大的工作环境、极少的培训和缺乏问责制是最应受指责的因素。
Often a manager's superiors can effectively encourage a manager's distasteful behavior when they fail to discipline the person's wrongdoings. Such workplaces are sometimes described as toxic. // 管理者的上级在对下属的错误行为不加管束时，实际上是在有效地鼓励管理者的不当行为。这类工作场所有时被称为有毒环境。
The second cause was attributed to the managers' characteristics. Those deemed to be most destructive were odd people — those without drive, those who allow personal problems into the workplace, and those with an unpleasant temperament or personality in general. // 第二个原因归因于管理者自身的特质。被认为最具破坏力的是那些怪人——缺乏进取心的人、把个人问题带入职场的人，以及脾气或性格普遍不好的人。
The third cause of poor management was associated with a deficiency of qualifications — not so much the formal variety one obtains from a university, but the informal variety that comes from credible work experience and professional accomplishments. // 第三个不良管理原因与资质不足有关——不是指从大学获得的正式学历，而是来自可信工作经验和专业成就的非正式资历。
The fourth cause concerned managers who've been promoted for reasons other than potential. One reason in particular why these people had been promoted was that they had been around the longest. It wasn't their skill set or other merits that got them the job; it was their tenure. // 第四个原因是那些因非潜力因素而被提拔的管理者。这些人被提拔的一个特别原因是他们在公司待的时间最长。让他们获得这份工作的不是他们的技能或其他优点；而是他们的资历。
A point worth making is that the study was based only on the perspective of employees. The researchers didn't ask senior leaders what they thought of their front line managers. // 值得一提的是，这项研究仅基于员工的视角。研究人员没有询问高层领导对一线管理者的看法。
It's quite possible they're content with how the individuals they promoted are now performing, merrily ignorant of the damage they're actually causing, which might explain why, as the researchers conclude, those same middle managers are usually unaware that they are a bad manager. // 他们很可能对自己提拔的人员目前的表现感到满意，却天真地不知道他们实际造成的损害，这也解释了为什么正如研究结论所言，那些中层管理者通常没有意识到自己是一个糟糕的管理者。
