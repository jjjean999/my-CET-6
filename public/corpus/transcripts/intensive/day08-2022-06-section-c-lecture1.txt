to be submissive, humble, grateful, and undemanding, show great pleasure when a doctor comes into your room, even if the visit is brief and useless. // 表现得顺从、谦卑、感恩、不苛求，当医生走进你的房间时要表现出极大的喜悦，即使这次探访简短而无用。
Don't challenge anyone with authority unless you're famous or very rich. // 除非你非常有名或非常富有，否则不要挑战任何有权威的人。
Those are a few strategies for dealing with today's American medical establishment. // 这些是应对当今美国医疗体制的一些策略。
What patients want is to be treated with respect and consideration. // 患者所希望的是得到尊重和体贴的对待。
But in my experience, too few hospitals and doctors are ready to do that. // 但据我的经验，太少有医院和医生愿意这样做。
In his book, A Whole New Life, novelist Reynolds Price recalls that his doctors chose a crowded hallway as the place to tell him he might have a tumor on his spinal cord. // 小说家雷诺兹·普赖斯在他的新书《全新生活》中回忆道，他的医生选择了一条拥挤的走廊作为告诉他可能患有脊髓肿瘤的地方。
It did not occur to the two physicians that a hallway was not the most appropriate place for that particular piece of news. // 这两位医生根本没有意识到，走廊并不是传达那个特定消息的最合适场所。
My surgeon who is in his mid-thirties looks tired. // 我的主刀医生三十多岁，看起来很疲惫。
He has been overwhelmed with patients who have fallen on the winter ice. // 他已经被那些在冬季冰面上滑倒受伤的病人压得喘不过气来。
He is a witty man, but sometimes his wit is unwelcome. // 他是个风趣的人，但有时他的风趣并不受欢迎。
"The health insurance company, Blue Cross, wants me to put you out in the snow tomorrow afternoon," he tells me after I have been in the hospital for more than a week. // "蓝十字健康保险公司想让我明天下午就把你扔到雪地里去，"他在我住院一周多后这样告诉我。
I'm terrified because I have no idea where to go. // 我感到非常恐惧，因为我根本不知道该去哪里。
I cannot walk or even lift my leg a few inches. // 我无法行走，甚至无法把腿抬起几英寸。
The hospital social worker strikes me as an idiot, but my complaints about her only annoy my surgeon. // 医院的社会工作者在我看来是个白痴，但我对她的抱怨只会让我的医生恼火。
"I have to work with these people," he tells my friend, doctor Karen Brudney, when she mercifully intervenes on my behalf and arranges for me to be transferred to another hospital. // "我得和这些人共事，"他告诉我的朋友——卡伦·布拉德尼医生。当时她好心为我出面干预，安排我转院到另一家医院。
"If you say one negative thing, they get defensive," she tells me later. // "如果你说一句负面的话，他们就会变得防御性很强，"她后来告诉我。
"They have this kind of institutional loyalty. Always bring in an advocate, that is, any other person with you to the hospital, and write down every single question and the answer, the name of every doctor and nurse. // 他们有一种制度性的忠诚。一定要带一个支持者陪同，也就是任何可以和你一起去医院的人，记下每一个问题和答案，每一位医生和护士的名字。
When people know you have their names, they behave better." // 当人们知道你掌握了他们的名字时，他们的表现会更好。"
"And," Brudney adds, "if you, as a patient, suggest that you might like to control even part of the situation or be consulted or informed, then you are considered difficult. // "而且，"布拉德尼补充说，"如果你作为患者暗示你可能想要控制部分局面，或者希望被咨询或知情，那么你就会被认为是个难搞的人。
They want you to be totally passive. The entire health care system, particularly hospitals and nursing homes, exists for reasons that have nothing to do with taking care of patients. // 他们希望你完全被动。整个医疗体系，尤其是医院和疗养院，其存在的原因与照顾患者毫无关系。
Patients are incidental." // 患者只是附带的。"
