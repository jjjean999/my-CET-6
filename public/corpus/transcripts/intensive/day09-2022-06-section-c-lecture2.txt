are probably teams you've worked with but you never want to work with again. // 有些团队你可能合作过，但再也不想与他们共事了。
But there must have also been other teams that you would prize reuniting with professionally. // 但肯定也有其他团队让你珍视与之重聚的专业关系。
In other words, your team had vitality. Vitality comes about when the ties people form with their fellow team members are such that they stay connected even after the team breaks up. // 换句话说，你的团队有活力。活力来源于团队成员之间形成的纽带——即使团队解散后，这种纽带依然让他们保持联系。
What characteristics of a team make its members more likely to stay in contact despite no longer working together? // 团队的哪些特征使其成员在不在一起工作后仍更有可能保持联系？
This question has been answered recently in a study published in a business journal. // 这个问题最近在一项发表于商业期刊的研究中得到了回答。
One of the two key factors the research team discovered is sameness. Specifically, sharing the same gender or ethnic origin. // 研究团队发现的两个关键因素之一是相似性。具体来说，就是相同的性别或种族背景。
The more members of a team share similar demographics, the more inclined they'll be to remain associates long after the team has served its purpose. // 团队成员之间的人口统计学特征越相似，在团队完成使命后，他们越倾向于继续保持联系。
After ties are established, similarity strengthens them. As a result, they regard these individuals with greater trust and mutual understanding, which motivates them to seek further opportunities for collaboration. // 建立联系后，相似性会加强这种纽带。结果是他们以更大的信任和相互理解看待这些人，这激励他们寻求进一步的合作机会。
In effect, people tend to create stronger and longer-lasting connections with similar others. // 实际上，人们倾向于与相似的人建立更强、更持久的联系。
Someone who looks and sounds different from us may have the resources we need to be more successful. Yet, we find them to be significantly less credible simply because they are different. // 外表和说话方式与我们不同的人可能拥有我们需要用来获得更大成功的资源。然而，仅仅因为他们不同，我们就认为他们明显不太可信。
If you are a fierce advocate of workplace diversity, you'll no doubt be horrified by such a revelation. // 如果你是职场多样性的坚定倡导者，毫无疑问你会对这样的发现感到震惊。
The second factor identified by the researchers is the quality of the relationships among the team members. // 研究人员确定的第二个因素是团队成员之间关系的质量。
The more they trust one another, share the same goals and depend on each other for the achievement of those goals, the stronger their chances of maintaining their connections, despite no longer working as one team. // 他们越互相信任、共享相同的目标并为实现这些目标而相互依赖，即使不再作为一个团队工作，他们保持联系的可能性就越大。
Teams with quality relationships have a shared belief that it's safe to take risks with each other, and their members are obliged to share the workload and help out. // 拥有高质量关系的团队有一个共同的信念：彼此承担风险是安全的，其成员有义务分担工作量、互相帮助。
From personal experience, I can see both the truth and the inconsistency of such studies. The truth is some of my closest friendships were formed as a result of having worked together on teams, and I actively seek opportunities to work with them again. // 从个人经验来看，我既能看到这类研究的真实性，也能看到其中的矛盾之处。真实之处在于：我最亲密的一些友谊正是因为团队合作而形成的，并且我积极寻求再次与他们合作的机会。
The inconsistency, though, is that I've never worked for a team more successful and cohesive than the one of which I am a member right now. And yet, the four of us have very little in common and are completely different demographically. So I am unlikely to question the value of a diverse workforce. // 然而，矛盾之处在于：我从未为一个比我现在所在的这个团队更成功、更具凝聚力的团队工作过。可是，我们四个人几乎没有共同点，在人口统计学特征上也完全不同。因此，我不太可能质疑多元化员工队伍的价值。
