recent study overturned what we think we know about lying. // 一项最近的研究颠覆了我们对撒谎的认知。
Most of us have a theory about how to tell if someone is telling a lie. // 我们大多数人都有一个关于如何判断某人是否在说谎的理论。
We may develop that theory from observations of those people we know well and see regularly, but we tend to generalize what we gather from that unscientific daily research and make it a universal theory. // 我们可能从对那些我们熟悉且经常见面的人的观察中形成这个理论，但我们往往把从这种非科学日常研究中收集到的信息泛化，使之成为一个普遍理论。
So we might imagine that liars have evasive eyes, or the opposite — they simply stare at you. // 所以我们可能会想象说谎者眼神闪躲，或者相反——他们只是死盯着你看。
Or perhaps it is more generally nervous behavior we associate with lies. // 或者也许是我们通常与谎言联系在一起的更普遍的紧张行为。
Whatever the particular theory, it's usually based on close observation of people we know. // 无论具体是什么理论，它通常基于对我们熟悉的人的密切观察。
And we get lots of practice. On average, we're lied to some 200 times per day. // 而且我们有大量的练习机会。平均而言，我们每天被欺骗大约200次。
These are mostly harmless lies, but lies nonetheless. // 这些大多是无恶意的谎言，但终究是谎言。
But there's a problem with our theories, even though they are based on all this observation. // 但我们的理论存在问题，尽管它们基于所有这些观察。
The average person — you and me — tested rigorously on how well we detect lies fails to do better than chance. // 普通人——你我——在接受关于识谎能力的严格测试时，表现并不比随机猜测好多少。
That's well established over many studies and lots of attempts by researchers to work out reliable ways to detect lies. // 这一点已被大量研究和研究人员寻找可靠识谎方法的多次尝试所证实。
It's even relatively easy to fool lie detectors, the gold standard of lie detection, by training yourself in breathing techniques and symptom suppression. // 通过训练呼吸技巧和抑制症状，甚至相对容易骗过测谎仪——这一识谎的金标准。
Is there any way to get better at detecting lies? The new research offers some surprising advice: Stop looking and listen instead. // 有什么方法可以提高识谎能力吗？新的研究给出了一些令人惊讶的建议：停止观察，改为倾听。
It turns out that if we're unable to see the face, but rather focus on the voice of the person in question, our accuracy rate improves considerably. // 结果表明，如果我们无法看到对方的面部，而是专注于对方的声音，我们的准确率会显著提高。
