achieve financial security, how much you save is always more important than the amount you earn or how shrewdly you invest. // 要实现财务安全，你存多少钱总是比你赚多少或投资多精明更重要。
If you are under 30 years old, your goal should be to save 20% of your monthly income after tax deductions. This is irrespective of how much you earn. // 如果你不到30岁，你的目标应该是将税后月收入的20%用于储蓄。无论你收入多少都是如此。
Approximately 50% should be reserved for essentials like food and accommodation. The remaining 30% is for recreation and entertainment. // 大约50%应该留作必需品（如食物和住宿）的开销。剩下的30%用于娱乐和休闲。
But for many young people, it'll be difficult to designate such a large proportion of their income for savings. If you find it hard to save any money at all, start by cutting all unnecessary spending. // 但对许多年轻人来说，将收入的这么大比例用于储蓄可能很困难。如果你发现很难存下任何钱，那就从削减所有不必要的开支开始。
Allocate a tiny amount of 1 or 2 percent for savings, and gradually increase that amount. Always keep that 20% goal in mind to prevent yourself from becoming complacent. // 分配1%或2%的微小金额用于储蓄，然后逐渐增加这个数额。始终牢记20%的目标，防止自己变得自满。
It can be challenging to stick to such a strict plan. But if you adopt the right mindset, you should be able to make it work for you. // 坚持这样一个严格的计划可能很有挑战性。但如果你采取正确的心态，你应该能够让它为你所用。
So, what should you be doing with the money that you're saving? Some must be kept easily accessible in case you need some cash in an emergency. // 那么，你应该如何使用你存下的钱呢？有些钱必须保持容易取用的状态，以防紧急情况下需要现金。

Dividends can be reinvested or used to purchase something you like. By following this plan, you should hopefully be able to enjoy your life now, and still be financially secure in the future. // 股息可以再投资或用来购买你喜欢的东西。遵循这个计划，你应该既能享受当下的生活，又能在未来保持财务安全。
