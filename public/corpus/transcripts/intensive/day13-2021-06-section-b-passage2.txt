the beginning of the twentieth century, on the Gulf Coast in the US State of Texas, there was a hill where gas leakage was so noticeable that schoolboys would sometimes set the hill on fire. // 二十世纪初，在美国德克萨斯州的墨西哥湾沿岸有一座小山，那里的天然气泄漏非常明显，以至于小学生们有时会放火烧山。
Pattillo Higgins, a disreputable local businessman, became convinced that there was oil below the gassy hill. Oil wells weren't drilled back then; they were essentially dug. // 帕蒂洛·希金斯，一个声名狼藉的当地商人，确信这座冒气的小山下有石油。那时油井还不是钻出来的，基本上是挖出来的。
The sand under the hill defeated several attempts by Higgins' workers to make a proper hole. Higgins had forecast oil at 1,000 feet, a totally made-up figure. // 山下的沙子多次挫败了希金斯工人挖掘合适井孔的尝试。希金斯预测石油在1000英尺深处，这个数字完全是编造的。
Higgins subsequently hired a mining engineer, Captain Anthony Lucas. After encountering several setbacks, Captain Lucas decided to use a drill, and his innovations created the modern oil drilling industry. // 随后希金斯聘请了一位采矿工程师——安东尼·卢卡斯上尉。经历了几次挫折后，卢卡斯上尉决定使用钻机，他的创新开创了现代石油钻井行业。
In January 1901, at 1,020 feet — almost precisely the depth predicted by Higgins' wild guess — the well roared and suddenly ejected mud and six tons of drilling pipe out of the ground, terrifying those present. // 1901年1月，在1020英尺处——几乎精确地就是希格斯胡乱猜测的深度——油井轰鸣起来，突然将泥浆和六吨钻井管道喷出地面，吓坏了在场的人。
For the next nine days, until the well was capped, the well poured out more oil than all the wells in America combined. // 在接下来的九天里，直到油井被封堵，这口井喷出的石油比美国所有油井的总和还多。
In those days, Texas was almost entirely rural, with no large cities and practically no industry; cotton and beef were the foundation of the economy. Higgins' well changed that. // 在那个年代，德克萨斯州几乎完全是农村，没有大城市，几乎没有工业；棉花和牛肉是经济的基础。希金斯的油井改变了这一切。
The boom made some探矿者 millionaires, but the sudden surplus of petroleum was not entirely a blessing for Texas. In the 1930s, prices crashed, to the point that in some parts of the country, oil was cheaper than water. That would become a familiar pattern of the boom-or-bust Texas' economy. // 这股热潮使一些探矿者成了百万富翁，但石油的突然过剩对德克萨斯州来说并非完全是一件好事。20世纪30年代，价格暴跌，在该国某些地区石油比水还便宜。这成为了德克萨斯州经济繁荣与萧条循环的熟悉模式。
