it's in the hands of animated polar bears or Santa Claus, there's one thing you'll find in nearly all ads for Coca-Cola: the characteristic glass bottle. // 无论是在动画北极熊还是圣诞老人手中，你几乎在所有可口可乐广告中都能看到一样东西：那标志性的玻璃瓶。
Most Americans don't drink soda out of the glass bottles seen in Coke's ads anymore. // 大多数美国人不再用可口可乐广告中看到的玻璃瓶喝汽水了。
But this week, the company is celebrating a century of the bottle that's been sold in more than 200 countries. // 但本周，公司正在庆祝这款瓶子问世一百周年——它已在200多个国家销售。
Flash back to 1915, when a bottle of Coca-Cola cost just a nickel. // 回到1915年，那时一瓶可口可乐只卖五美分。
As the soft drink gained in popularity, it faced a growing number of competitors — counterfeits even trying to copy Coke's logo. // 随着这款软饮料越来越受欢迎，它面临着越来越多的竞争对手——甚至有仿冒者试图抄袭可口可乐的标志。
So, according to Coca-Cola historian Ted Ryan, the company decided to come up with packaging that couldn't be duplicated. // 因此，据可口可乐历史学家泰德·瑞安介绍，公司决定设计一种无法被仿制的包装。
A product request was sent to eight different glass makers. Workers at the Root Glass Company got the request and began flipping through the encyclopedia at the local library, landing on cocoa seed. // 产品需求被发送给八家不同的玻璃制造商。鲁特玻璃公司的工人收到需求后，开始在当地的图书馆翻阅百科全书，最终查到了可可豆。
Though cocoa seed is not an ingredient of the soda, they designed their bottle based on the seed's shape and large middle. // 虽然可可豆不是这种汽水的成分，但他们根据可可豆的形状和硕大的中部设计了他们的瓶子。
It won over Coke executives in Atlanta and would go on to receive its own trademark, spur collections and earn Coca-Cola an iconic image that made it part of American culture for a century. // 它赢得了亚特兰大可口可乐高管的青睐，随后获得了自己的商标，引发了收藏热潮，并使可口可乐成为一个标志性形象，成为美国文化的一部分长达一个世纪之久。
It was 100 years ago this week that the bottle earned a patent. // 就在本周的一百年前，这款瓶子获得了专利。
By World War II, Coke bottle sales had ballooned into billions. // 到第二次世界大战时，可口可乐瓶的销量已激增至数十亿。
Americans mostly consume Coke out of aluminum or plastic today, but the glass bottle remains a symbol of America that's readily recognized around the world. // 如今美国人大多用铝罐或塑料瓶饮用可口可乐，但玻璃瓶仍然是美国的象征，在全世界广为人知。
