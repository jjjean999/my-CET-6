M: It's my last day at work tomorrow. I'll start my new job in two weeks. My human resources manager wants to conduct an interview with me before I leave. // 男：明天是我在这份工作的最后一天。两周后我就要开始新工作了。人力资源经理想在我离开之前对我进行一次面谈。
W: Ah, an exit interview. Are you looking forward to it? // 女：啊，离职面谈。你期待吗？
M: I'm not sure how I feel about it. I resigned because I've been unhappy at that company for a long time, but I'm not sure if I should tell them how I really feel. // 男：我不确定自己是什么感觉。我辞职是因为在那家公司不开心很久了，但我不确定是否应该告诉他们我的真实感受。
W: To my way of thinking, there are two main potential benefits that come from unleashing an agitated stream of truth during an exit interview. The first is release. Unburdening yourself of frustration, and perhaps even anger to someone who isn't a friend or close colleague can be wonderfully freeing. // 女：在我看来，在离职面谈中倾泻出积压的真相主要有两个潜在好处。第一个是释放。向一个不是朋友也不是亲密同事的人倾诉你的挫败感，甚至愤怒，可以让人感到无比轻松。
M: And let me guess. The second is that the criticism will, theoretically, help the organization I'm leaving to improve, making sure employees of the future are less likely to encounter what I did? // 男：让我猜猜。第二个好处是，从理论上讲，批评意见将有助于我离开的组织改进，确保未来的员工不太可能遇到我所遭遇的情况？
W: That's right. But the problem with the company improvement part is that very often it doesn't happen. An exit interview is supposed to be private, but often isn't. In my company, the information gained from these interviews is often not confidential, and the information is used as dirt against another manager, or can be traded among senior managers. // 女：没错。但公司改进这部分的问题在于，这种情况往往不会发生。离职面谈本应是保密的，但往往并非如此。在我的公司里，从这些面谈中获得的信息往往不是保密的，这些信息被当作攻击其他经理的把柄，或者可以在高管之间进行交易。
M: Now you've got me rethinking what I'll disclose in the interview. There is always a chance that it could affect my reputation and my ability to network in the industry. It is a pretty small industry after all. // 男：现在你让我重新考虑我要在面试中透露什么了。这总是有可能影响我的声誉以及我在行业内建立人脉的能力。毕竟这是一个相当小的行业。
W: Anything you initially gain from the instant satisfaction of telling it like it is, you might lose down the track by injuring your future career prospects. // 女：你从直言不讳中获得的即时满足感，日后可能会因为损害你的职业前景而失去。
M: Right. Perhaps it would be better getting things off my chest by going to one of those rate-your-employer websites. // 男：说得对。也许去那些给雇主评分的网站上发泄一下会更好。
W: You could. And don't do the interview at all. Exit interviews are not mandatory. // 女：你可以那样做。或者干脆不做这个面谈。离职面谈并不是强制的。
