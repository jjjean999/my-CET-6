M: Today, I'm talking to the renowned botanist, Jane Forster. // 男：今天，我们要对话的是著名植物学家简·福斯特。
W: Thank you for inviting me to join you on the show, Henry. // 女：谢谢你邀请我来参加你的节目，亨利。
M: Recently, Jane, you've become quite a celebrity since the release of your latest documentary. Can you tell us a little about it? // 男：最近，简，自从你最新的纪录片发布以来，你已经成为相当有名的人物了。能给我们介绍一下吗？
W: Well, it follows my expedition to study the vegetation indigenous to the rain forest in equatorial areas of Southeast Asia. // 女：嗯，它记录了我去东南亚赤道地区雨林研究当地特有植被的考察之旅。
M: You certainly get to travel to some very exotic locations. // 男：你确实去过一些非常充满异国情调的地方。
W: It was far from glamorous, to be honest. The area we visited was accessible only by canoe and the living conditions in the hut were primitive, to say the least. There was no electricity, and our water supply was a nearby stream. // 女：说实话，这远没有那么光鲜亮丽。我们去的地方只能乘独木舟到达，小屋里的生活条件至少可以说是很原始的。那里没有电力，我们的水源来自附近的一条小溪。
M: How were the weather conditions while you were there? // 男：你们在那里的时候天气状况怎么样？
W: The weather was not conducive to our work at all, since the humidity was almost unbearable. At midday, we stayed in the hut and did nothing. It was too humid to either work or sleep. // 女：天气完全不利于我们的工作，因为湿度几乎让人无法忍受。中午我们就待在小屋里什么都不做。太潮湿了，既没法工作也没法睡觉。
M: How long did your team spend in the jungle? // 男：你们的团队在丛林里待了多久？
W: Originally, we planned to be there for a month. But in the end, we stayed for only two weeks. // 女：原本我们计划在那里待一个月。但最后只待了两周。
M: Why did you cut the expedition short. // 男：为什么你们缩短了考察时间？
W: Halfway through the trip, we received news that a hurricane was approaching. And we had to evacuate at very short notice. // 女：行程进行到一半时，我们收到消息说有一场飓风正在逼近。我们不得不在很短的时间内紧急撤离。
M: That sounds like a fascinating anecdote. // 男：听起来是个很有趣的经历。
W: It was frightening. The fastest evacuation route was through river rapids. We had to navigate them carrying all of our equipment. // 女：那很可怕。最快的撤离路线要穿过河流的急流区。我们必须携带所有装备穿越急流。
M: So overall, was the journey unsuccessful? // 男：那么总的来说，这次旅程不成功吗？
W: Absolutely not. We gathered a massive amount of data about the local plant life. // 女：当然不是。我们收集了大量关于当地植物的数据。
M: Why do you put up with such adverse conditions? // 男：你为什么要忍受这样恶劣的条件呢？
W: Botany is an obsession for me. Many of the destinations I visit have stunning scenery, and I get to meet a variety of people from all over the world. // 女：植物学对我来说是一种痴迷。我去过的许多目的地都有令人惊叹的风景，而且我能结识来自世界各地的各种各样的人。
M: So where will your next destination be? // 男：那么你的下一个目的地会是哪里？
W: I haven't decided yet. // 女：我还没决定。
M: Then we can leave it for another occasion. Thanks. // 男：那我们留到下次再聊吧。谢谢。
